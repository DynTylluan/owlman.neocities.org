>GEORGE A. ROMERO

Zombies are not a new concept, they have been
around for 100s of years, infact, the word "zombie"
is was first recorded in the 1819 book - _History
of Brazil_ by poet Robert Southey.

In 1932, Victor Halperin directed White Zombie, a
horror film starring Bela Lugosi. In this
movie, the zombies are more seen as slaves rather
than the flesh-eating monsters we think of today.

We very much have cult film maker George A. Romero
to thank for the creation of the modern-day zombie.
Night of The Living Dead is strongly considered to
be the birth of the modern day zombies. The movie
centres around a group of survivors who are forced
to make do in an abandoned house. While I won't
spoil any of the plot to you.

I will say that one of the main characters, Ben
(played by Duane Jones) is a black man, while
casting a non-white person in a movie today is
not seen as a big thing, you must
remember that this movie was being made at the same
time as the African-American Civil Rights Movement
was in full swing. I personally think that Ben is
the best character, just because he is one of the
only level-headed people in the movie.
 
While I like the male characters, the female
characters are just poorly written, Barbara, for
example, is just a crap character, whose main job
seems to be running away from things, crying and
getting in the way. I like a good heroin as the next
person, but Barbara is just shite.

To be fair to the movie, Barbara has a very small
roll in the grand scheme of things, but still, she
does annoy me.

I am writing this as the director of Night of The
Living Dead, has sadly passed away at age 77.

Quoting The Los Angeles Times

Romero died Sunday in his sleep after a "brief but

>BABY DRIVER REVIEW

In issue two of OwlMail I talked about what I
thought about the trailer of Baby Driver, and to be
honest, I was unsure about what the movie was going
to be like, I hadn't seen any reviews and I didn't
even watch the Your Movie Sucks' review of the
film.

After going to the pictures on the 20th of July, I
am happy to report that Edgar Wright has once
again, made a classic!

Spoilers I guess...

The Good:
>The soundtrack kicks arse on a whole new level

>The main character is very likeable

>The action sceans are really cool (and gave me a
boner) especially the one at the start

>cars r kewl B-)

>The lyrics of some of the songs can be seen
written on street objects

>Samm (Brogan Hall) wasn't annoying

The Bad:

Queen tracks
